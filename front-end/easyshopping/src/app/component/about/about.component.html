At the University

My academic journey at the University of Virginia was a dynamic one marked with my willlingness to explore multiple paths. I started my journey pursuing a degree in Mechanical Engineering, later moved on to Computer Engineering until I finally found my passion in Computer Science.

During my time at the University of Virginia, I immersed myself in a wide range of experiences that helped me grow both as an engineer and as an individual. I undertook coursework,club activities internships, and club activities that have collectively helped me to hone my abilities.

Coursework

Some of the highlights of my coursework were "Introduction to Machine Learning", "Programming and Data Representation" , "Web Programming Languages", and "Database Management".

"Introduction to Machine Learning" gave me an opportuntiy to develop Machine Learning Algorithms, prior to the release of ChatGPT. I had never really given artifical intelligence much of a thought, but the course really opened my eyes to what would end up being one of the biggest developments in software. When ChatGPT was relased in the final quarter of 2022, I felt I was already ahead in terms of understanding the technology, thanks to the foundation I had built while taking this class at the University of Virginia.

"Programming and Data Representation" was a class that I hated taking while I was in the class, but I will forever be grateful to haven taken. It covered topics which many students would consider 'dry' as it primarily focused on the foundations of programming and how code is translated down into something hardware could understand. Although I didn't fully understand its importance at the time, I later developed a deep appreciation for the class, as it played a pivotal role in establishing my foundations as a Software Engineer.

"Web Programming Languages" and "Database Management" were two courses that I found particularly intriguing. What interested me the most was the relationship between them. "Web Programming Languages" delved into the realms of PHP and JavaScript, equipping me with the skills to build dynamic and interactive web applications. Surprisingly, this knowledge played a key role when I took "Database Management," where I navigated the intricacies of MySQL. The synergy between the two courses reinforced how web applications and databases are connected, making it an experience I am very grateful for.

Other Experiences

I also had the opportunity to gain hands-on experience through internships that were critical in the progression of my career. My time at a local startup called HackCville, now known as Forge, introduced me to the world of web development. I got exposed to React (both JavaScript and Native) and JavaScript. In this dynamic environment, we operated in an agile setup, conducting daily stand-up meetings to track our team's progress. This was my first exposure to the collaborative and fast-paced agile atmosphere.

Another valuable internship allowed me to explore mobile development. I had the task of analyzing application patterns in both iOS and Android apps, ultimately creating a script for automating the process of developing standardized ("cookie cutter") apps. This experience, although mostly independent, involved regular reporting to my supervisor.

Beyond my formal coursework and internships, my passion for gaming led me to join the UVA Student Game Development club. Using Unity as our game engine and C# as our coding language, I embarked on a two-year journey creating games. I gained significant experience collaborating within a team and even took on a leadership role in my final year.
